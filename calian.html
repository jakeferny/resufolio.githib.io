Calian

Spacecraft Engineering Student

May - December 2023

St. Hubert, Quebec


Calian is responsible for flying the Canadian Space Agency's (CSA) satellites and is located at the John H. Chapman Space Centre. As the first student hired by Calian, I was exposed to many projects and tasked with the training of future students. I paved the path for future collaborations between Calian and AlbertaSat. This relationship is still developing and Calian hopes to start developing its operations technology for CubeSat teams across the country.
My role with Calian mainly involved developing data managment systems for satellite telemetry as well as anamoly detection tools. I developed APIs using the Flask framework in python to more easily search for relevant information and highlight abnormal values and build reports. This made a job that usually takes hours be completed perfectly in less than a minute.

Within my time at the CSA I had the chance to work on important satellite monitoring tasks on Canada's biggest space missions, such as SciSat, NEOSat, and the RADARSAT missions. I worked closely on interdisiplanary teams from members across a wide variety of backgrounds. I presented to government and private interested parties, and was given responsibilties that full time employees would take on. I was known as a reliable and fast worker, who found and took on projects out of my own volition. I found the work interested and was eager to add my part to the growing space industry in Canada.
